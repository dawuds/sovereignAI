# Global Policy and Regulatory Landscape

Sovereign AI is being shaped by a rapidly evolving patchwork of national regulations, trade policies, and infrastructure investments. This document surveys the major jurisdictions.

---

## European Union

### EU AI Act

The world's first comprehensive AI regulation, imposing a **risk-based classification** framework:

| Risk Level | Examples | Obligations |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Unacceptable | Social scoring, real-time biometric surveillance | Prohibited |
| High | Critical infrastructure, law enforcement, hiring | Conformity assessments, transparency, human oversight |
| Limited | Chatbots, deepfakes | Transparency obligations |
| Minimal | Spam filters, AI-powered games | No obligations |

Applies to any organization that deploys AI in the EU market — regardless of where the organization is headquartered. Phased enforcement began August 2024 with full compliance required by August 2026.

Source: [European Commission AI Strategy](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/european-approach-artificial-intelligence)

### Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA)

Scheduled for Q1 2026, CADA aims to:
- Triple EU data center capacity within seven years
- Create a common framework for public sector cloud procurement
- Establish binding requirements for sovereign cloud usage

Built on Article 114 TFEU (internal market harmonization) — would have direct effect across all 27 member states.

Source: [EU Cloud AI Act](https://www.eu-cloud-ai-act.com/)

### Gaia-X

Launched as the "Airbus of the Cloud" by France and Germany to build a European cloud ecosystem based on open standards. As of March 2025: 180+ data spaces in implementation. However, it has been criticized as a "paper monster" (Nextcloud CEO) and European cloud market share continued to decline despite the initiative. AWS, Microsoft, and Google were admitted as members — diluting the sovereignty purpose.

**Structural challenge:** Three US-based companies account for **65% of EU cloud services**, with ~90% dependency on US cloud infrastructure overall.

Source: [Polytechnique Insights](https://www.polytechnique-insights.com/en/columns/digital/gaia-x-the-bid-for-a-sovereign-european-cloud/)

### EuroHPC AI Factories

EUR 750M EU investment (matched by member states for **EUR 1.5B total**) to establish seven "AI Factories" across Europe — shared GPU clusters for startups, SMEs, and researchers. Additional sites selected in Austria, Bulgaria, France, Germany, Poland, and Slovenia. France and Germany plan to federate systems into a **virtual 50,000-GPU supercomputer**.

Source: [EuroHPC JU](https://www.eurohpc-ju.europa.eu/ai-factories_en)

### AI Continent Action Plan (April 2025)

EU-wide strategy to make Europe a global AI leader while safeguarding democratic values. Emphasizes trustworthy AI, open-source models, and European data spaces.

## United States

### AI Diffusion Rule

Originally signed January 15, 2025 (Biden administration), creating a **three-tier global licensing regime** for AI chip exports:

| Tier | Countries | Access |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Tier 1 | 18 close allies (Five Eyes, Japan, Netherlands, South Korea, Taiwan) | Essentially unrestricted |
| Tier 2 | Most other countries | Conditional — license required above thresholds |
| Tier 3 | Restricted/embargoed nations (China, Russia, Iran, North Korea) | Blocked |

**Trump administration rescission (May 2025):** Replaced the blanket framework with a more targeted, transactional approach — including conditional export of NVIDIA H200 chips to China with a **25% fee**. A new final rule formalized January 15, 2026, implements flexible license review.

Source: [BIS Press Release](https://www.bis.gov/press-release/department-commerce-announces-rescission-biden-era-artificial-intelligence-diffusion-rule-strengthens), [Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/01/15/2025-00636/framework-for-artificial-intelligence-diffusion)

### CHIPS Act

Drives domestic semiconductor manufacturing with funding for Intel, TSMC, and Samsung US-based fabs. Aims to reduce US dependency on Asian chip fabrication for national security.

### RESTRICT Act

Proposed legislation to prohibit advanced AI chip sales to China while creating a license-free export pathway for trusted US companies operating data centers abroad.

Source: [Congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R48642)

### Export Enforcement

Active enforcement against sanctions evasion:
- 42 PRC entities added to export lists (March 2025), another 23 (September 2025)
- DOJ dismantled a China-linked smuggling network that moved **$160M in restricted NVIDIA chips**

Source: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/12/31/160-million-export-controlled-nvidia-gpus-allegedly-smuggled-to-china.html)

## China

### Regulatory Framework

China's AI regulation remains **fragmented** across multiple laws for different AI use cases. 2025 is described as a "transitional period" — a centralized comprehensive AI law remains uncertain.

Key existing regulations:
- **Algorithmic Recommendation Regulations** (2022)
- **Deep Synthesis (Deepfake) Regulations** (2023)
- **Generative AI Regulations** (2023)
- **Data Security Law** and **Personal Information Protection Law**

Source: [Global Legal Insights](https://www.globallegalinsights.com/practice-areas/ai-machine-learning-and-big-data-laws-and-regulations/china/)

### Self-Reliance Strategy

Beijing has made **"independent and controllable" AI** a key national objective:
- State-linked enterprises discouraged from deploying US chips, even "compliant" ones
- Aggressive domestic chip development: Huawei Ascend, Cambricon, Hygon
- DeepSeek endorsed Huawei Ascend, accounting for **18–22% of all Ascend chip purchases** in early 2025
- DeepSeek V4: trillion-parameter multimodal model supporting Huawei CANN natively

Source: [Merics](https://merics.org/en/report/chinas-drive-toward-self-reliance-artificial-intelligence-chips-large-language-models), [Carnegie Endowment](https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2025/07/chinas-ai-policy-in-the-deepseek-era?lang=en)

### Global AI Governance Framework (July 2025)

China released its own framework calling for multilateral cooperation on AI governance — positioning itself as an alternative standard-setter to the US/EU axis.

## ASEAN / Malaysia

### Malaysia

Malaysia launched **Southeast Asia's first sovereign full-stack AI infrastructure** in May 2025.

Key developments:
- **National AI Office (NAIO)** launched December 2024 — sovereign AI is one of seven working groups
- **National AI Roadmap 2021–2025 (AI-RMAP)** developed by MOSTI and MDEC
- **PDPA amendments** (phased 2025): Phase 1 (Jan) administrative changes; Phase 2 (Apr) biometric data protections; Phase 3 (Jun) mandatory DPO and breach notification
- **140 AI solution providers** generating MYR 1 billion in revenue
- **NVIDIA $4.3B** AI data center development in Malaysia

Source: [Chambers & Partners](https://practiceguides.chambers.com/practice-guides/artificial-intelligence-2025/malaysia/trends-and-developments), [FPF](https://fpf.org/blog/malaysia-charts-its-digital-course-a-guide-to-the-new-frameworks-for-data-protection-and-ai-ethics/)

### Singapore

The regional governance leader:
- **Model AI Governance Framework for Generative AI** (2024, updated 2025 with OECD/GPAI criteria)
- **Model AI Governance Framework for Agentic AI** (January 2026)
- **AI Verify** — open-source AI testing toolkit
- **ISAGO** — self-assessment guide for organizations
- Leads the **ASEAN Working Group on AI Governance (WG-AI)**
- AI Safety Institute (AISI) collaborates internationally

Source: [IMDA](https://www.imda.gov.sg/resources/press-releases-factsheets-and-speeches/press-releases/2026/new-model-ai-governance-framework-for-agentic-ai)

### ASEAN Regional

- **ASEAN Guide on AI Ethics and Governance** (February 2024) with supplementary Generative AI guide (2025)
- **Vietnam** became first SE Asian country to promulgate an **AI Law** (December 2025, phased enforcement from March 2026)

## India

### IndiaAI Mission

Cabinet-approved March 2024. Goals:
- Democratize access to computational resources
- Foster indigenous AI capabilities
- Attract and retain AI talent
- Support AI startups
- Ensure ethical AI development

Source: [PSA India](https://www.psa.gov.in/ai-mission)

### BHASHINI

India's multilingual AI platform — **35+ languages, 1,600+ AI models, 18 language services**. Successfully migrated from a global hyperscaler to **Yotta's sovereign Government Community Cloud and Shakti Cloud** (NVIDIA H100), achieving:
- **40% performance boost**
- **30% cost savings**
- Validated at Maha Kumbh 2025 serving real-time translation in 11+ Indian languages

Source: [PIB India](https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=2225350&reg=3&lang=1)

## Comparative Regulatory Approaches

| Approach | Champion | Philosophy | Trade-off |
|----------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Regulation-first** | EU | Set rules before scale | May slow innovation but protects rights |
| **Investment-first** | UAE, Saudi Arabia | Build infrastructure, regulate later | Fast deployment but governance gaps |
| **Balanced** | Singapore | Governance frameworks + investment | Measured but may miss first-mover advantage |
| **Self-reliance** | China | Domestic alternatives at all costs | Accelerated by export controls but quality gaps |
| **Voluntary** | Japan, US | Industry self-regulation | Fastest innovation but least protection |
